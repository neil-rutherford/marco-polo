The Process

Here's the process in a nutshell:

  1. You get your work visa application materials in order.
  2. You find a job in China.
  3. You apply for a tourist visa.
  4. You go to China.
  5. Your employer takes your work visa application materials and uses them to get you a work permit.
  6. You take your work permit to Hong Kong and apply for a work visa.
  7. You take your work visa back to China and apply for a residence permit.

You may be wondering why we don't have you just apply for a work visa. The short answer is that it is much easier and cheaper to complete the work permit application process in China.

The long answer?

All of the documents that you procure for your work visa application have a six-month time limit on them. If you try to submit an authenticated police clearance that is more than 6 months old, for example, it will be rejected and you will have to go through that process again. That would be frustrating, wouldn't it?

Now imagine sending documents back and forth across the Pacific. If you use the cheap option, it will take your package 1-2 weeks to arrive (each way). If you use express shipping, it will get there in 3 days. Since you're using our service, we assume that (1) you are looking for a job, and (2) you are already paying thousands of dollars to get to China. Chances are you're not going to want to spend more money on express, cross-ocean shipping.

Now imagine how much time it takes to navigate Chinese bureaucracy and red tape. Need we say more?

We have designed our process to have you touch down in China 44 days after you sign up, if all goes to plan. This is so that you can communicate better with your employer, cut down on wait times, and not have your documents expire while you navigate red tape. You are not expected to work on a tourist visa; you are expected to be getting a work permit and work visa.
