Chinese cyberspace is strictly controlled by the government. Any website that the Chinese government cannot control/censor is blocked in China. This includes (but is not limited to):

  • Google services: Search, Gmail, Maps, Calendar, App Store
  • Social media networks: Facebook, Twitter, Reddit, Instagram, Youtube
  • Apps that use infrastructure made by blocked companies: Spotify, Tinder, Soundcloud (all use Facebook to log in)
  • News outlets: New York Times, Washington Post, CNN, Wall Street Journal
  • Blogging platforms: Tumblr, Wordpress

A VPN makes it look like you are accessing the internet from another country, which allows you to access sites that are blocked in China. This will allow you to stay connected with the outside world and contact your family. If you are interested in learning more, here is an article about VPNs and digital censorship in China.
